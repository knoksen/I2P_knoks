# I2P Connect Digital Autonomy Doctrine

I2P Connect exists to make private communication over I2P feel normal, hopeful, useful, and community-driven.

The project treats privacy as dignity, not spectacle. It should help beginners understand what their tools are doing without fear marketing, conspiracy framing, or unsupported guarantees.

## Principles

- Privacy should feel calm, practical, and understandable.
- Local user control comes before growth loops, cloud convenience, or analytics.
- Security claims must be measured, testable, and documented.
- Responsible use is part of the product, not a legal footnote.
- Beginner onboarding should reduce confusion without hiding limitations.
- Community trust is earned through open documentation, small validated releases, and honest language.
- I2P-native design should respect I2P's strengths and constraints instead of forcing mainstream communication expectations onto it.

## Product Tone

Prefer:

- privacy as dignity
- hope over fear
- calm, beautiful UX
- responsible use
- open-source trust
- simple onboarding
- local-first design
- least-privilege architecture
- honest limitations

Avoid:

- conspiracy framing
- fear marketing
- criminal symbolism
- hacker-only aesthetics
- unsupported anonymity claims
- unsupported encryption claims
- promises of Zoom-like real-time video over I2P
- phone-network replacement language
- emergency-use claims
- exposing I2P control ports or sensitive services publicly

## Honest Limits

I2P Connect must not promise that it can make every user anonymous, safe, untraceable, compliant, or protected from all adversaries.

Future releases may provide private messaging features, app-layer cryptography, readiness checks, and I2P transport integration. Each claim must become release-facing only after implementation, tests, and documentation exist.
